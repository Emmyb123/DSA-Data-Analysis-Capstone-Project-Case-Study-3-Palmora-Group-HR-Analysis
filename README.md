# Palmoria HR Analysis

## Project Overview  
This project provides an HR analytics review of Palmoria Group, focused on gender equality, salary distribution, regional pay compliance, and bonus allocation. The goal was to identify areas where the company can improve fairness, meet regulatory standards, and enhance employee satisfaction. The analysis was done using Power BI.

## Key Findings  

### Salary Band Distribution  
- The company’s salary structure shows a wide distribution of earnings.  
- The majority of employees earn between $50,000 and $90,000, with 292 employees earning over $90,000.  
- A total of 654 employees earn below the required minimum of $90,000, indicating non-compliance with the regulation. Only 292 employees meet or exceed the required minimum.  

### Salary Band by Region  
- The majority of the employees earn over $90,000, with 292 employees in this highest salary band distributed across all three locations (Abuja: 97, Kaduna: 111, Lagos: 84).  
- Kaduna has the highest concentration of high earners in the over $90,000 bracket, while the lower salary bands ($20,000-$80,000) show relatively balanced distribution across locations, indicating that higher-paying positions are more concentrated in the Kaduna region.  

### Gender Distribution  
- The workforce is composed of 49.15% males, 46.62% females, and 4.23% undisclosed employees.  
- The gender distribution across locations shows a similar pattern of male dominance in all three regions, with males consistently outnumbering females across Kaduna (182 vs 165), Abuja (159 vs 158), and Lagos (124 vs 118).  
- Kaduna has the largest workforce with 361 total employees and shows the most significant gender imbalance, while Abuja demonstrates the most balanced gender distribution with nearly equal representation (159 males vs 158 females).  
- The gender distribution across departments reveals significant variation, with some departments showing male dominance, others female majority, and a few achieving near parity.  
- Departments like Legal (49 males vs 34 females), Support (42 males vs 35 females), and Sales (40 males vs 36 females) demonstrate male dominance.  
- Services (42 females vs 37 males) and Human Resources (41 females vs 38 males) show a female majority, suggesting certain functional areas may be more attractive to or accessible for female employees.  
- Engineering (38 females vs 36 males) and Marketing (31 females vs 33 males) are relatively balanced.  

### Average Salary by Gender and Gender Versus Department  
- The average salary for males is approximately $75,000, while for females it is $72,000. Employees with undisclosed gender identity have the highest average salary at approximately $78,000.  
- The salary analysis reveals consistent gender pay gaps across most departments, with males systematically earning higher average salaries than females.  
- Some exceptions exist, such as Marketing where females earn slightly more ($79K vs $74K for males).  
- Marketing and Accounting emerge as the highest-paying departments, while Product Management shows the lowest combined salary levels.  
- The undisclosed gender category generally falls between male and female salary ranges across departments, indicating a systemic compensation issue requiring management intervention.  

### Average Rating by Gender  
- The distribution of performance ratings is similar between genders, with males receiving 465 total ratings and females 441 ratings.  
- The slight difference suggests balanced performance assessment participation, with 40 undisclosed gender employees also receiving ratings.  

### Bonus Pay by Region  
- The compensation analysis reveals significant regional disparities. Kaduna leads in bonus distribution with $0.83M, followed by Abuja at $0.80M, and Lagos at $0.57M.  
- Kaduna is the highest-compensated region with $27.5M in total pay, followed by Abuja at $24.9M, and Lagos at $19.5M.  
- This gap raises questions about pay equity and may reflect differences in role levels, cost of living adjustments, or regional strategies.  
- Palmoria Group has allocated a total of $2.199M in bonus payments company-wide, demonstrating a significant investment in performance-based compensation.  

## Conclusion  
Palmoria Group’s compensation and demographic data highlight several areas for immediate management action: addressing gender-based pay disparities, ensuring compliance with wage regulations, and reviewing regional bonus allocation to promote fairness. These insights can guide policy reviews and targeted interventions to strengthen equity and employee satisfaction.
